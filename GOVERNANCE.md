# Governance

Frontier Max is a benchmark interpretation and workload-policy layer. It does not create benchmark questions, modify source scores, or proxy inference content.

## Decisions

- Benchmark transformations require visible methodology, source provenance, and tests.
- Routing-policy changes require a public rationale, primary-source evidence, and tests.
- Workload profiles define objectives and constraints; there is no universal model rank.
- Sponsored placement, provider margin, and affiliate economics are excluded from analytical and routing objectives.
- Content capture remains off by default. Any future outcome sharing must be separately consented to and content-free.

## Releases

Every release is versioned and reproducible from tagged source. Material changes to model eligibility, evidence selection, fallbacks, attribution, or receipt behavior require release notes.

## Conflicts

Maintainers disclose relevant provider, benchmark-publisher, infrastructure, and funder relationships and recuse when a conflict could reasonably affect a decision.
