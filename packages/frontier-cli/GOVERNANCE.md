# Governance

Frontier Max is a policy layer, not a benchmark publisher or inference proxy.

## Decisions

- Routing-policy changes require a public rationale, primary-source evidence, and tests.
- Source benchmark values are preserved; transformations must be visible and reproducible.
- Workload profiles define objectives and constraints. There is no universal model rank.
- Sponsored placement, provider margin, and affiliate economics are excluded from routing objectives.
- Content capture remains off by default. Any future outcome sharing must be separately consented to and content-free.

## Releases

Every release is versioned, carries its policy manifest, and can be reproduced from the tagged source. Material changes to model eligibility, signals, fallbacks, attribution, or receipt behavior require release notes.

## Conflicts

Maintainers disclose provider, benchmark-publisher, or infrastructure relationships relevant to a decision and recuse when the conflict could reasonably affect route policy.
